Bottom Line Up Front

ITA 1.0 is a snapshot system. It gives precise recommendations based on a fixed point-in-time view of a client's return, cannot accept new inputs outside its data model, runs on a planner written in a domain-specific language that only two engineers at Intuit can read, and incorrectly triggers (or fails to trigger) AMT — a failure that has caused a real client to pay $20,000 in excess advance tax.

ITA 2.0 replaces the computational core with an LLM tax engine tested to dollar-exact accuracy against ProConnect. It accepts natural language inputs — with a smaller intentional data model (that potentially supports MCP access into Intuit's existing tax engines where required). RAG retrieves the right rates and thresholds for the right tax year. Strategy-specific guardrails prevent hallucination. Multi-year projections, state returns, and entity modeling route to Lacerte via MCP. The platform shift allows ITA to become what it was always meant to be: the advisor tool that sees the whole return, models the interactions between strategies, and tells the professional what a client would actually save — not what each strategy would save in isolation.

Why the architecture is the problem

ITA 1.0 evaluates strategies independently. Real tax returns do not work this way. An S-corp conversion reduces SE tax — but simultaneously reduces QBI, costing up to 20% of the deduction. A retirement contribution may save far more than its face-value tax reduction by crossing an IRMAA threshold or unlocking a QBI deduction that the phaseout was suppressing. A Roth conversion pushes AGI up and may make Social Security more taxable, phase out other deductions, or trigger NIIT — raising the real marginal cost to 2–3× the stated rate.

Rules-based systems that evaluate in isolation will overstate benefits when interactions reduce savings, and miss value entirely when interactions amplify savings. In both cases the client is harmed — once by a bad recommendation, once by an opportunity never surfaced.

Problem Statements

D4D Framework

Customer Problem — Independent Tax Professional (PTG)
I am an independent tax professional managing 200+ clients with complex returns — self-employed income, S-corps, real estate, retirement accounts.
I am trying to give each client a credible, quantified tax plan that accurately reflects what they will actually save — not what each strategy saves in isolation.
But the tool I use evaluates strategies one at a time, surfaces numbers I cannot explain when clients ask where they came from, and rejects inputs that don't fit its model.
Because the calculation engine does not model interactions between strategies, and the input layer assumes the current tax return is complete and correct.
Which makes me feel exposed — I am recommending numbers the tool can't justify, and I know the client's actual outcome may differ materially from what I showed them.
Customer Problem — QBL Tax Expert
I am a QuickBooks Live tax expert delivering advice to SMB owners who have never worked with a tax professional before.
I am trying to quickly produce a tax plan grounded in the client's actual financial situation, explained in language they understand.
But the tool requires me to manually locate and enter financial details from documents the client shares, and gives me numbers without explaining the logic or flagging interactions.
Because the input model is form-based and expert-dependent — it was designed for CPAs who already know what fields mean, not for a consultative expert conversation.
Which makes me feel like the software is working against the conversation, not inside it.
Business Problem — ITA Product & Intuit
I am the ITA product team responsible for making ITA the leading tax planning tool for professionals across PTG and QBL.
I am trying to grow ITA's attach rate, NPS, and revenue contribution by making it the most trusted source of tax planning quantification.
But the current engine cannot model strategy interactions, cannot accept natural language inputs, cannot be extended to multi-year or entity modeling, and cannot be safely maintained by the team that now owns it.
Because the architecture is irreparably constrained — the DSL is vendor-unsupported, AMT is dependent on PTO state that ITA cannot read, and the input model is a static approximation of what clients' returns actually contain.
Which makes me feel that every roadmap item we ship on top of ITA 1.0 is adding debt to a foundation that cannot hold the weight of what the market now requires.
Ideal State

In a Perfect World

Vision A tax professional describes their client's situation in plain language — income, entity structure, goals, recent changes — and ITA produces a complete, accurate, multi-strategy plan showing exactly what the client will net after all interactions are accounted for. The professional can iterate by asking "what if they delay the Roth conversion to next year?" and ITA updates every number instantly, flagging all the downstream effects. Plans can be projected three years forward, include state tax, and handle S-corps and C-corps without switching to a different tool. Every number on the plan can be traced back to a specific section of the return — and every recommendation comes with an explanation the professional can share with their client in confidence.

The biggest benefit to the professional: they can stop presenting approximate numbers based on isolated strategies and start presenting verified, interaction-aware plans they can defend to clients, to the IRS, and to themselves.

The biggest benefit to Intuit: a feedback loop — every plan produced in ITA against a PTO return teaches the engine something new. The accuracy validates itself in production, and every validation makes the moat wider.

Feature Areas

Six Feature Areas

ITA 2.0 is built around six capability areas. The first two form the platform foundation. The remaining four are built on top and depend on the foundation being correct.

Feature Area 01
LLM Tax Engine (Core)
The calculation foundation. An LLM-based engine that accepts structured or natural language inputs, computes the full 1040 including all interactions (QBI, AMT, NIIT, IRMAA, Social Security), and produces a dollar-exact output validated against ProConnect. Shadow mode enables safe production rollout with fallback to the deterministic system.
Feature Area 02
Natural Language Input Layer
Replaces the static field-based input model with a conversational interface. Professionals describe client situations in plain language; ITA extracts structured inputs, flags ambiguities, and mutates the session context when new information is provided. Includes PDF intake (1040 and supporting docs) and PTO data pull.
Feature Area 03
Strategy Skills & Guardrails
Per-strategy guardrails that define the complete set of return items each strategy must affect. Prevents hallucination by specifying required outputs. Human-approved skill files updated when validation loop detects a delta; makes every new strategy a structured, auditable addition to the platform.
Feature Area 04
Multi-Year & Entity Modeling (Lacerte MCP)
Routes multi-year projections, state returns, S-corp, C-corp, and partnership scenarios to Lacerte via MCP. ITA orchestrates the request and surfaces results in its own interface. Solves in one integration what would take years to rebuild natively.
Feature Area 05
RAG: Rates, Limits & Thresholds
A retrieval layer that provides the engine with correct rates, contribution limits, phaseout ranges, and IRMAA tiers for the relevant tax year. Eliminates the LLM's known failure mode of confusing parameters across years. Updated annually; internally maintained by the ITA tax content team.
Feature Area 06
Plan Summary Builder (PSB) 2.0
The advisor-facing output surface. Shows the full tax picture, strategy interactions, net savings (not isolated headline savings), and Safe Harbor lines. Strategies can be toggled on/off with instant recalculation. Client-ready summaries generated in plain language. Sessions saved and retrievable.
Functional Requirements

FA-01 · LLM Tax Engine

ID Priority Requirement Definition of Done
1.1 P0 Dollar-exact 1040 federal accuracy LLM tax engine output matches ProConnect Tax on 1040 Federal within ±$1 on tax liability for a benchmark suite of ≥200 anonymized returns covering: W-2 + self-employment + S-corp K-1 + rental income scenarios. AMT, QBI, NIIT, and SE tax all within tolerance.
1.2 P0 Strategy interaction modeling When multiple strategies are selected, the engine computes a unified tax liability — not the sum of individual strategy savings. Net saving = baseline liability − combined-strategy liability. Each interaction effect (QBI reduction, IRMAA tier, Social Security taxability) is surfaced separately in the output.
1.3 P0 AMT correct triggering AMT computation uses parallel calculation path. AMT is triggered if and only if AMT exceeds regular tax. Does not depend on PTO state. Validated against ≥25 AMT-applicable returns in benchmark suite. Conforms to current-year exemption amounts and phaseout ranges via RAG.
1.4 P0 Shadow mode with fallback LLM engine runs in parallel with ITA 1.0 deterministic system. User sees deterministic output until accuracy threshold (≥98% match on benchmark suite) is met and shadow mode is disabled by product decision. Delta logging captures every disagreement for review.
1.5 P0 PTO return data ingestion Engine accepts structured data from ProConnect Tax via ProConnect Data Service API. All relevant line items (income, deductions, credits, carryforwards) parsed and mapped to engine input schema. Round-trip accuracy tested: PTO data → engine → liability matches PTO-computed liability.
1.6 P1 Validation feedback loop When engine output disagrees with PTO return, delta is logged and surfaced to the tax content team. Learning captured as a candidate update to the relevant strategy skill file. Human approval gate required before skill file update is deployed to production. Feedback cycle target: <5 business days per reviewed delta.
1.7 P1 Latency SLA Single-strategy tax calculation: p95 ≤ 4 seconds. Full multi-strategy interaction recalculation: p95 ≤ 8 seconds. Measured end-to-end from input submission to displayed output. Lacerte MCP calls excluded from ITA latency SLA (shown as async).

System Architecture

ITA 2.0 Platform Architecture

Four layers compose the ITA 2.0 platform. Each layer has a clear interface, can be validated independently, and can be evolved without breaking the others.

Layer 01 · Input
Natural Language Input & Data Pull
Sources: PTO via ProConnect Data Service API · 1040 PDF via OCR (digital + scanned) · TTO via authorized API (legal gate) · Direct NL conversation

Output: Structured session context object — all input fields confirmed by professional, with source attribution per field.
Layer 02 · Knowledge
RAG: Rates, Limits & Thresholds
Contents: Tax brackets by filing status & year · Contribution limits (IRA, 401k, SEP, DB) by year · IRMAA tiers by year · QBI phaseout ranges · NIIT threshold · AMT exemptions · MACRS tables

Maintained by: ITA tax content team · Updated annually before filing season · Version-tagged by tax year.
Layer 03 · Computation
LLM Tax Engine + Strategy Skills
Engine: LLM with full 1040 federal calculation capability · Validated dollar-exact against ProConnect · Shadow mode with deterministic fallback

Skills: Per-strategy guardrail files · Define required output fields & interaction dependencies · Human-approved update workflow · Versioned and auditable.
Layer 04 · Extension
Lacerte MCP (Multi-Year & Entity)
Handles: Multi-year projections (1–5 years) · State returns (all 50) · S-corp & C-corp entity modeling · Partnership K-1 projections

Integration: MCP protocol · ITA passes session context; Lacerte returns structured projection results · Async call pattern · Results surface in PSB.
Why this architecture, and not rebuilding native Multi-year projections and state returns could in theory be built natively into ITA 2.0. The Lacerte MCP approach solves two problems simultaneously: (1) it makes these capabilities available at GA without a 2+ year native build, and (2) Lacerte already has this accuracy validated — rebuilding it would require revalidating it. The MCP integration is not a shortcut; it is the right answer given the tools that exist and the timeline that matters.

Validation Loop

The Validation Loop as Moat

The production feedback loop is not just a quality control mechanism — it is Intuit's primary competitive advantage in AI-driven tax planning. No competitor without access to ProConnect's return data can build the same validation signal.

1

Plan Generated in ITA

Professional uses ITA to produce a tax plan for a client who also has a ProConnect return. Session context, inputs, and calculated outputs are logged against an anonymized client ID.

2

Return Filed via ProConnect

Client files via ProConnect Tax. Actual filed return data is available via ProConnect Data Service. Final computed liability, AMT, QBI, and all relevant fields are captured.

3

Delta Computed

ITA engine output for the planning session is compared against the filed return. Every field that disagrees beyond a tolerance threshold is logged as a delta. Strategy and input characteristics tagged for attribution.

4

Candidate Skill Update Generated

For each delta pattern, a candidate update to the relevant strategy skill file is generated and queued for review. The LLM explains the discrepancy and proposes the correction.

5

Human Approval Gate

A tax content team member (CPA/EA) reviews the candidate skill update, validates the proposed correction, and approves or rejects. Rejected updates go back to the LLM with a reason code. Approved updates are versioned and deployed.

6

Engine Improves

Next time that strategy type + input pattern is encountered, the engine runs the updated skill file and produces a more accurate output. The accuracy improvement compounds across all clients with similar scenarios — Intuit's network effect applied to tax accuracy.

Why this is a moat The validation loop requires access to both the planning intent (ITA session) and the actual filed return (ProConnect). No competitor without both a planning product and a filing product can build this signal. Every ProConnect return filed by an ITA-using professional makes the engine more accurate — and makes the gap between ITA 2.0 and any competing planning tool wider.

Availability
99.5% uptime SLA excluding planned maintenance. Deterministic fallback available when LLM service degraded. Session persistence: no data loss on browser close, network interruption, or server restart. RTO: <15 minutes for P0 incidents during filing season.
Accuracy
Tax Calculation
Dollar-exact (±$1) vs. ProConnect on benchmark suite of ≥200 returns at GA. Accuracy regression test runs on every engine deployment. Any regression >0.5% of suite fails the deployment gate. Shadow mode delta tracking feeds weekly accuracy report to PM and tech lead.
Legal & Compliance
Regulatory
All output labeled as advisory. IRS Circular 230 compliant. No output purports to constitute a filed return or a legal tax opinion. Disclaimer reviewed and approved by Intuit Legal before launch. State-specific disclaimers added for states with unique advisory regulations.
Scalability
Filing Season Load
Sustain 10× average daily request volume during Jan 15–Apr 15. Auto-scaling configured for LLM inference tier. Lacerte MCP calls queued and rate-limited to protect Lacerte's SLA. Load test completed ≥4 weeks before Jan 15 each year.
Success Framework

Goals & Success Metrics

ITA 2.0 success is measured at three levels: the North Star (client outcome), input metrics (product behavior that drives the outcome), and guardrails (what must not regress). Targets are for the 12-month post-GA period unless noted.

Type Metric Description Target
North Star Net Tax Saving Delivered per Client Median dollar saving across all ITA 2.0 planning sessions where a strategy was recommended and the client filed via ProConnect. Captures the full interaction-aware net saving, not headline savings. ≥$2,000/client median; 20% increase over ITA 1.0 baseline
North Star Professional Trust Score (NPS) NPS among PTG professionals who used ITA 2.0 for ≥3 client plans. Separates "trust in the numbers" from "ease of use" — both measured independently in the survey. NPS ≥ +40 (vs. ITA 1.0 baseline of +N/A — to be established in alpha)
Input LLM Engine Accuracy Percentage of shadow mode comparisons where LLM output matches ProConnect-filed return within ±$1 on total tax liability. Tracked weekly by strategy type. ≥ 98% overall at GA; ≥ 99% for P0 strategies at V1.1
Input Input Complaint Rate Percentage of ITA 2.0 support tickets and NPS verbatims mentioning input problems (missing fields, can't update values, bundled inputs). Measured as share of all feedback volume. Reduce from 75% (ITA 1.0) to < 20% within 6 months of GA
Input NL Input Adoption Rate Percentage of new planning sessions initiated via natural language input (vs. form-fill). Proxy for professional comfort with conversational intake. ≥ 50% of new sessions at 90 days post-GA
Input Plans per Professional per Month Average number of completed planning sessions per active PTG professional per month. Measures workflow efficiency improvement — faster plans → more clients served. 25% increase over ITA 1.0 baseline at 90 days post-GA

Guardrail AMT False Trigger Rate Percentage of benchmark returns where ITA 2.0 triggers AMT incorrectly (false positive) or fails to trigger it when it should (false negative). The $20K harm case was an AMT false positive. 0% on benchmark suite. Any production report of a false trigger treated as P0 incident.
Guardrail Skill Hallucination Rate Percentage of strategy recommendations that surface without satisfying the strategy skill file output signature. Measures effectiveness of guardrail enforcement. 0% at GA. Skill validation is a hard gate — not a metric with a tolerance band.
Guardrail Data Incident Rate Number of security incidents involving customer financial data processed by ITA 2.0 in a rolling 12-month period. 0 P0/P1 data incidents. Any P0 incident triggers an immediate rollback to shadow mode.
Guardrail Session Context Integrity Percentage of session save-and-retrieve operations where the restored context matches the saved state exactly (all input values, all calculated outputs, all confirmation states). 100% fidelity on save/retrieve. Any data loss on resume treated as P0 bug.

Scope

In Scope / Out of Scope

In Scope — ITA 2.0 V1.0

  • LLM tax engine for 1040 Federal (all income types, deductions, credits)
  • Shadow mode with deterministic fallback
  • Strategy skill guardrails for P0 use cases (UC-1 through UC-6)
  • RAG layer: rates, limits, thresholds (federal, current tax year)
  • Natural language input extraction (PTG and QBL professionals)
  • PTO data pull via ProConnect Data Service (LG-1.1a)
  • Chat-driven context mutation (LG-1.7)
  • Session save and retrieve (LG-1.8)
  • PSB 2.0: net saving display, Safe Harbor line, strategy toggle
  • Lacerte MCP integration for multi-year projections
  • Client-ready plain-language summary export (PTG workflow)
  • Validation feedback loop (delta logging + human-gated skill updates)
  • AMT correct triggering (parallel calculation, no PTO dependency)

Out of Scope — V1.0 (Roadmap Candidate)

  • State tax calculation natively within ITA (routed to Lacerte)
  • S-corp / C-corp / partnership entity modeling natively (Lacerte)
  • TTO data pull (LG-1.1c) — pending legal and privacy gate
  • 1040 PDF intake (LG-1.1b) — targeted for V1.1
  • Supporting document intake (K-1s, 1099s) — V1.1
  • DIY filer (TurboTax) direct access — not a V1.0 persona
  • Credit Karma tax planning integration — separate initiative
  • International (non-US) tax returns
  • Payroll tax computation (separate product domain)
  • Automated plan delivery to clients without professional review
  • Strategy skill files for UC-7 through UC-13 (V1.1 target)
  • Real-time IRS rule change monitoring and automated RAG update
Scope rationale V1.0 scope is deliberately constrained to what is validated or near-validated per the Project AIR learning plan. State returns, entity modeling, and PDF intake are real needs but their V1.0 absence is offset by Lacerte MCP (state + entity) and PTO data pull (replaces most PDF intake for existing ProConnect clients). TTO data pull is excluded pending a legal and privacy gate that is a genuine blocker, not a product choice.
